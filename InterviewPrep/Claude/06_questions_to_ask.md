# Questions to Ask Your Interviewer

> Always have 2–3 ready per round. Good questions signal seniority and genuine interest. Tailor to who's in front of you.

---

## FOR THE HIRING MANAGER
- What does the DevOps/Platform team own end-to-end — where do your responsibilities start and stop vs other teams?
- What are the biggest reliability or delivery challenges the team is facing right now?
- What does success look like for this role in the first 6–12 months?
- How is the team structured — embedded in product teams, or a central platform team?
- What's the on-call model and how healthy is it (alert volume, rotation)?

## FOR ENGINEERS / PEERS (technical round)
- What does your CI/CD and deployment flow look like today? Any pain points?
- What's your deployment strategy — rolling updates, canary, blue-green? How do you measure canary health?
- How do you handle autoscaling — HPA on what metrics (CPU, custom, external)? Do you run Cluster Autoscaler?
- How mature is your IaC / GitOps adoption?
- What's your observability stack, and how do you handle incidents/postmortems?
- What's the biggest piece of tech debt the team wants to tackle?
- Cloud provider(s) and how much is managed vs self-hosted?

## FOR SRE-SPECIFIC ROLES
- How do you define and track SLOs? Do you use error budgets to gate releases?
- What's the balance between ops toil and engineering/project work?
- How do you run postmortems — blameless? What changed after the last big incident?

## ABOUT GROWTH / CULTURE
- How does the team support growth toward staff/principal or architecture tracks?
- How are technical decisions made — RFCs, design reviews, consensus?
- What keeps you (the interviewer) here?

## ABOUT THE ROLE LOGISTICS (recruiter / late stage)
- What's the interview process from here and the timeline?
- Team's remote/hybrid/on-site expectation?
- How is this role positioned in terms of level/band?

---

## AVOID ASKING (this early)
- Comp/leave specifics with engineers (save for recruiter/offer stage).
- Anything easily Googleable about the company.
- "Do you have any concerns about me?" unless you handle the answer well.

## PRO TIP
Ask **one follow-up** to their answer — shows you're actually listening, not reading a list.
